What is GDPR?
Introduction
The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is the EU's comprehensive data protection law, effective May 25, 2018. It governs how organisations collect, process, store, and share personal data of EU/EEA residents — regardless of where the organisation is located.
Key Principles
GDPR is built on 7 principles: Lawfulness & Transparency · Purpose Limitation · Data Minimisation · Accuracy · Storage Limitation · Integrity & Confidentiality · Accountability. As a Customer Success team member, principles 1, 3, and 7 are most directly relevant to your daily work.

Key Definitions Under GDPR
Understanding GDPR starts with its vocabulary. Three terms will come up in nearly every customer interaction: personal data, data subject, and data controller.
Personal data is any information that can identify a living individual — directly (name, email) or indirectly (IP address, cookie ID, combination of attributes).
Consider adding a real-world example here: "For CS teams, this includes customer email addresses, support ticket history, and even call recordings stored in your CRM." This grounds the definition in your audience's daily context.
A data subject is the identifiable person the data belongs to — in your context, almost always a customer or end user. A data controller is your company, which determines the purpose and means of processing that personal data.
The term "data processor" is missing here — third-party tools like Salesforce or Zendesk that process data on your behalf. Adding this prevents a common misconception among CS teams.
